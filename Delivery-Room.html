Delivery Room Instruments/Tools

No. Name Image Use
01 Mayo Table Mayo stands are portable instrument stands with a tray top used to hold surgical instruments and materials during operating room and in-office procedures.
02 Cord Clamp The umbilical cord clamp is a medical device used to hold the cord in place when the cut (surgery) is made, and the clamp may stay on for several days while the remaining cord attached to the baby dries.
03 Suture Chronic gut is processed to provide great resistance to absorption. Surgical gut suture is indicated for use in general soft tissue approximation and/or ligation, including use in opthalmic procedures, but not for use in cardiovascular and neurological tissues.
04 Sterile Gloves Sterile gloves are a type of disposable glove that are devoid of any germs and free from all microorganisms. They aid the prevention of wound infections and limit the danger of blood and bodily fluid pathogen exposure for healthcare professionals.
05 Mayo Table Cover/Drape The drapes are sufficient length to drape the top of a mayo table and also provide adequate protectionalong the upright support(vertical arm).
06 Patient Drape The primary purpose of draping the surgical patient is to isolate the surgical site from the other areas of a patient's body and nonsterile areas of the OR table in order to contribute to reducing the risk of surgical site infection(SSI).
07 Plastic Placenta Bowl Use to receive the placenta of a mother after the delivery of the baby.
08 Needle Holder Used to hold a surturing needle during surgical procedures.
09 Mayo Scissors Used to cut the umbilical cord.
10 Kelly Clamp Curve/Straight Used to clamp the cord towards the baby/placenta.
11 Metz Scissor Used to cut suture during episiorraphy.
12 Tissue Forceps Used to grasp tissue.
13 Handle with 10/20 blades Used to cut superficial tissue or skin.
14 Kocher (Ochsner) Used to grasp heavy tissue and can be used as a clamp.
15 Babock Forceps Used to grasp delicate tissue such as intestine, fallopian tube, ovary, appendix, also available in long sizes.
16 Allis Forcep Used to grasp tissue and is available in long sizes.
17 Backhaus Towel Clip/Clamp Used to hold drapes, most especially towels in place.
18 Hemostat Forcep Used to clamp blood vessels or tag sutures.
19 Thumb Forceps TThey look like tweezers. They can be short or long and are tapered and have serrations or groves at the tips.
20 Ovum Forceps Commonly used to remove placental fragments inside the uterus.
21 Mosquito Forceps Used to clamp small blood vessels. May be straught or curved.



OB/GYN Tools

No. Name Image Use
01 Speculum(Cusco's(Graves' Speculum) To visualize vaginal walls and cervix during pelvic exams, pap smears, or procedures.
02 Uterine Sound Measures the depth and direction of the uterine cavity, usually before inserting an inauterine device Iv.
03 Tenaculum(Pozzi Foreps) Grasp and stabilize the cervix during procedures like (UD insertion or dilation and curretage).
04 Vulsellum Forceps Hold the uterus or cervix during surgeries like hypersectomy or DC.
05 Sim's Speculum A single-bladed speculum used for examining the vaginal canal and cervix, often with the patient in Sims position.
06 Doyen's Retractor Retract the abdominal wall or uterus during C-sections or gynecologic surgeries.
07 Ovum Forceps Remove products of conception during D&C or for removing polyps in the uterus.
08 Amniotic Hook (Amnihook) Artificially rupture the amniotic sac (amniotomy) during labor to induce or augment labor.
09 Pinard Fetoscope A manual tool used to auscultate fetal heart sounds, particularly in low-resource settings.
10 Fetal Dropper Detects fetal heartbeat during pregnancy check-ups.
